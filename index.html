DConf 2013 Call for Submissions

We invite you to submit session proposals for DConf 2013, the conference of the D programming language (Menlo Park, CA, May 1-3 2013).

The D programming language is undergoing rapid growth, spurred by a bustling talented community and downright furious coding activity on the open-source compiler front-end and standard library. These also fuel the gdc compiler (based off of the gcc infrastructure) and the ldc compiler (grown atop of the llvm infrastructure). A conference to catalyze the community was just bound to happen, and indeed we have successfully funded DConf 2013 via Kickstarter.

DConf is the main face-to-face event for everyone and everything related to the D language and environment. DConf 2013 will feature leading speakers from the D community. For people who want to get into D but are not yet familiar with it, DConf 2013 also features a full-day pre-conference tutorial held by Walter Bright and Andrei Alexandrescu.

We are hereby inviting members of the D community to submit session proposals on any topic of interest. Such include, but are not limited to:

  • The core language
  • Standard library
  • Libraries
  • Tools
  • Programming techniques and idioms
  • Generic and generative programming using D, including Domain Specific Languages
  • Concurrency and parallelism using D
  • Experience reports
  • Vision and mission
  • Deployment

We'd like to emphasize that the list above is not complete by any means. Anyone with any level of D knowledge is a potential candidate for a great talk around an interesting topic. D has benefitted enormously from a diverse, grass-roots effort, and DConf should do the same.

Best Presentation Award

DConf 2013 will offer a Best Presentation Award based on attendee votes.

Session Formats

DConf has an open structure meaning the format is yours to suggest. Here are some popular options:

  • Conference talk, 60 or 90 minutes
  • Short talk, 15 or 30 minutes
  • Blitz talk, 5 minutes
  • Panel or Birds-of-a-Feather discussion, 60 minutes
  • Design Contest or Coding Jam, 60-90 minutes

Submitting a Proposal

Please submit proposals to Walter Bright via email at dconf2013@digitalmars.com. Each proposal should include:

  • Title
  • Type of session: presentation, tutorial, panel, ...
  • Duration (could be a range if adjustable)
  • Abstract: a brief (3-8 sentences) paragraph describing the topic covered.
  • Level: basic, intermediate, advanced.
  • Benefits: what will the attendee gain by attending the proposed session?
  • A brief (20-50 words) author biography.
  • Author agrees with video recording: yes or no.

Please include sufficient information to allow the committee to judge the value of your submission. First-time authors are encouraged to include extended credentials.

If the proposal is accepted, all session materials (such as slides and code) will be made available publicly.

Admission

We operate on a tight budget and absolutely any proceeds left, if any, at the end of this year's conference will be rolled into funding the next year's edition. Therefore we encourage speakers to help us keep costs down wherever possible (e.g. by asking their employers to cover travel costs). That being said, sharing knowledge takes a lot of work and ought to be correspondingly appreciated. Upon admission speakers will benefit of:

  • Free admission to the conference.
  • Reduced price admission to the optional pre-conference tutorial.
  • Up to three nights at the conference hotel.
  • North America airfare up to $450.

We have a limited overflow budget for covering international airfare, and many of our best contributors live abroad, so we'll do our very best to accommodate you needs. Bottom line, if you have a great topic in mind, send it over and we'll work something out, even if you're from Mars!

 

Good luck!
Andrei Alexandrescu and Walter Bright (Conference co-chairs)
